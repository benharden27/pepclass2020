Chemical Oceanography is concerned with the the study of the interaction of dissolved ions, molecules, gasses in the ocean. This is a diverse field and we will be covering the basics of macronutrients, oxygen and carbon in this class. This will be a necessarily broad overview.

Reading

Read the following from Segar:

  • Chapter 5:
    • The Dissolving Power of Water (pg. 94-95)
    • Sources and Sinks of Chemicals Dissolved in Seawater (pg. 95-96)
    • Salinity (pg. 96-97)
    • Dissolved Chemicals in Seawater (pg. 97-101)

  • Read The Ocean: A Carbon Pump which talks about the various ways in which carbon dioxide gets from the surface to the deep ocean including through the photosynthesis of phytoplankton.

  • Watch this short video about the Biological Pump

  • Read The Threat of Acidification to Ocean Ecosystems, which introduces Ocean Acidification and the threats it poses.
